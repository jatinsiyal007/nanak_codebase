To stimulate convergence action for promoting good governance, NANAK introduced a programme

-The National Forum for Action on Convergence (NFAC) - for institutionalizing convergence, enhancing participatory processes and norms and building capacity development for leadership roles at various levels.

Women Empowerment

For the empowerment of women, NANAK has set up vocational centers for women in Bihar, Delhi and Maharashtra

To provide vocational training, health awareness, self-defense training, legal literacy, and counseling as well as build overall capacities for income generating opportunities.

Strengthening Governance

Working at the grassroots, we understood that it was necessary to strengthen Panchayati Raj Institutions,

which are the lowest level of self-governance in India to bring about real changes and recommend relevant policy recommendations.

About Us

In 2013, the Network and Alliance for Non-Profit Activities and Knowledge (NANAK) was formed with the vision to empower the socially disadvantaged, by harnessing the full potential of individuals as well as institutions — primarily through knowledge creation and dissemination. With the larger goal of converging micro and macro level policy interventions, NANAK strives to initiate meaningful changes in order to inform policy action by undertaking direct actions at the grassroots as well as bridging the gap between the government and the most marginalized and vulnerable communities.

While the primary change-makers of the organization remain our members from the rural landscapes in Bihar and the economically disadvantaged in Delhi-NCR region, NANAK is also supported by a team of highly dedicated and passionate volunteers, professionals, motivators and advisors who are driven by their commitment and zeal to bring about a positive change in society. Today, as a national level non-profit organization, NANAK has membership base in 8 states of India.
